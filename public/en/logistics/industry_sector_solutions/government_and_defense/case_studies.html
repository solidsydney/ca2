Governments and Defense Case Studies

A logistics partner big enough to deliver freight of any kind, to any place via air, ocean, road or rail, but capable of giving its customers the personal attention they need. Welcome to DHL’s Governments & Defense Services.

The II Marine Expeditionary Force, Military Working Dog Platoon, Camp Lejeune, NC called on DHL Governments & Defense Team to discuss a first time special mission to move Military Working Dogs to Alpha Company Headquarters Battalion Provost Marshals Office/Military Working Dogs Twenty-Nine Palms, CA for special missions training. These dogs are trained in explosive detection which searches for explosives or explosive residue; Narcotic detection which searches for numerous types of drugs; and Tracker dogs. The dog’s names are "Amber", Belgian Malinois, "Anca", Sheppard, and "Speedy", Black Lab. Their personal medical and training records had to be hand carried and turned over via signature required and not revealed to anyone. The dogs had to travel under control temperature requirements and had to be checked on during connecting flights. This mission was accomplished as required by the Military Provost, Camp Lejeune.

The 306th Psychological Operations (PSYOPS) Company called DGF into action when they realized that they were days away from the Exercise Key Resolve/Foal Eagle 2009 training in Korea and their Mobile Command Units where sitting idle in Los Alamitos, CA. DGF had the Dangerous Goods specialists recon the vehicles in Alamitos, secure space with the air carrier and pre-advise our South Korean import department of this important shipment in anticipation of receiving the green light from the 306th company as the window of opportunity continually shrank. While communicating directly with the 306th, we learned that the troops arrived in Korea on March 6th and required their vehicles for the exercises that commenced on the weekend. Our team wasted no time in recovering and tendering the cargo to Korean Airlines while satisfying all document requirements for Dangerous Goods and US Customs by day’s end. The shipment was delivered to Camp Humphreys in a flawless effort thanks to the involvement and cooperation of the DGF Governments & Defense Team. Providing vital equipment to our troops overseas enabled our military to focus on operational exercises that are designed to train the U.S. and Republic of Korea forces. DGF is proud to have contributed to the transportation of key equipment for our U.S. Forces and allies training in the Pacific Rim.

The M&M Distribution Center collaborated with the Governments & Defense Team to ship more than 25,000 lbs of candy to the troops and their families to Bahrain, Guam, and Japan just in time for Halloween. Roberta and Terry remained flexible during this move and ensured the chocolates were shipped in specialized refrigerated containers so that when they left the M&Ms distribution center frozen they would slowly thaw enroute and be ready to go on the shelf upon arrival. This was not the normal move for our team as it did not involve heavy armor or spare parts but a mission is a mission and the troops moral and welfare are of the utmost importance especially during any holiday.

DHL was contacted by the American Embassy of Paraguay to deliver 22 tons of syringes to ASU for the Ministry of Health. These syringes were vital in administrating the vaccine for Yellow Fever before it becomes an epidemic; therefore had to get to their destination PRONTO! DGF pulled this time critical shipment off by working strategically to ensure the syringes were delivered safely to the Ministry of Health warehouse with an overnight service from PHL to ASU.

After DHL Governments & Defense Team received a request from the Submarine Command in Norfolk, VA about the possibility of flying a mini sub to the Seychelles islands we assembled a team to route a plan and successfully load and deliver the sub to Seychelles in a week for the Navy! No doubt the team faced some challenges along the way but with each individual’s expertise, dedication, and willingness to work together another successful mission was completed for our US Navy.

Residents of Haiti were already facing food shortages and rising inflation rates when triple natural disasters hit. Hurricanes Gustav, Hanna and Ike drenched Haiti causing widespread flooding, massive mudslides and hundreds of fatalities. Responding to the massive flooding disaster in Haiti the international humanitarian organization, Operation Blessing International (OBI) has launched major relief and recovery efforts which include purchasing and importing a portable water purification system. OBI provided the water purification unit, all logistical implementation, customs clearances, in-country implementation of the unit, and hands-on training. DHL provided free shipping of the system from OBI headquarters in Virginia Beach to Port au Prince, Haiti. Called the Living Water Treatment System, the unit will produce 12,000 gallons of filtered water per day, which can serve an entire village. The system can be used for up to 5 years with no filter replacement costs. OBI, with support from DHL, has previously deployed Living Water Treatment Systems to disasters in Mexico, Bangladesh, Bolivia, Peru, and Ecuador.

It all started with an email from Major Dave Sandvold, Team Leader of MiTT 0731 (Military Transition Team) stationed north of Al Qaim along the Syrian border. Major Dave says that when asked by Americans of what they can provide, he always suggests items that will keep his Marines healthy and any items that will increase the quality of life for the children of Iraq. After all, nothing is more popular in Iraq than soccer. In a combined effort with a mass emailing force from CISCO Veteran Employees, American Legion, The USO, DHL and The American Youth Soccer Organization (AYSO) answered the call to gather and ship 5000 soccer balls to Major Dave for the Iraqi children. DHL made a contribution to this effort by providing the logistics of 3500 soccer balls from Los Angeles to Balad, IQ and 1500 to Bagram, AF. The Marines are going overseas to stabilize the region, and with the donated soccer balls, they hope to also bring peace to the areas they visit, one child at a time.

For the Troops is a non profit group who started their work out of a garage with about 300 volunteers helping to sort, pack and ship boxes to our brave American Heroes. The organization had been blessed with donations of items to fill the boxes and the volunteers to help stuff them but in these hard economic times the donations have been plentiful but the money to ship has slowed down.

For the Troops then contacted DHL Governments & Defense Team requesting help with sending care packages to the front line troops, those who face the most hardship while performing their jobs. With the help of DGF office donations, For the Troops was able to send 10,000 care packages to our freedom fighters in Iraq and Afghanistan.

If you know someone deployed in Iraq or Afghanistan, For the Troops is willing to send them a care package. You may contact usa@forthetroops.us for your request.

DHL joins forces to secure E.J. Metals contract to ship fire fighting protection vehicles for the troops. E.J. Metals; a manufacturer of rapid intervention fire trucks, rescues, tankers, pumpers and wild land vehicles is providing these fire fighting capable Kubota’s to the U.S. Military. They were awarded a significant contract to provide 25 Kubota’s to the US ARMY at Bagram Air Base to provide fire fighting protection for our troops in Afghanistan.

This is the second shipment of 5 vehicles and 5 spare parts crates for this contract of 25 vehicles and 25 spare parts kits.

Javelin Missile System Command Launch Units, or CLU’s, are critical pieces of equipment on today’s battlefield. When a CLU becomes inoperable it can often take weeks to acquire, repair and return it to the warfighter in the field. In the heat of battle, this timetable is simply not acceptable...a solution was needed. DHL Government & Defense Services teamed up with Raytheon / Lockheed Martin Javelin Joint Venture and the US Army’s Close Combat Weapons Systems to solve this problem. Working closely with these groups, DHL developed a system, working with several worldwide DHL entities, to execute highly secure and extremely fast transit for critical assets deployed in any combatant zone in the world…safe, secure and rapid transit managed by the largest single provider of foreign military services globally…DHL Governments & Defense Services.

During routine operations Jan 09 in the Pacific Ocean, the USNS Rainier experienced problems with the propeller shaft and couldn’t maneuver through the water. The ship managed to get to Santa Rita Guam dry dock for emergency repairs. The Military Sealift Fleet Support Command, who is responsible for all shipboard repairs, called DHL for assistance to ship a shaft and associated repair parts to get this ship back on its Mission. Since there aren’t any freighters that service Guam, DGF Governments & Defense Team had to hot shot this freight to Osaka, Japan where a B727 was waiting to uplift these parts into Guam right away.

A huge wave of relief has landed in the Philippines thanks to some key Operation Blessing partners; DHL being one of them. DGF, who has partnered with OBI in previous disaster responses and medical missions, has offered to fly over $151,000 worth of emergency medicines into the capital Manila which was over 80 percent submerged with floods after being hit by two typhoons in just one week.

The medicine will help complement the efforts of OBI team members who have already reached more than 100,000 victims with relief such as emergency food and water, medical care and cleanup assistance. OBI is one of the largest charities in America, providing strategic disaster relief, medicine aid, hunger relief, clean water and community development in 22 countries around the world on a daily basis.

DHL is a key partner with OBI in times of crisis and disaster, providing emergency shipments of water purification systems, medicine and other aid to assist OBI’s relief efforts in places such as Bangladesh, Mexico, Honduras and Ecuador and now the Philippines. By being able to expedite shipments, clear customs and provide in-country logistical support, DHL plays a significant role in helping OBI continue to be a first responder in crisis.

The US ARMY decided they would provide additional motivation for their football team this year by including their biggest and fiercest Buffalo (MRAP) Vehicle at the Army-Navy game in Philadelphia which took place Saturday December 12 2009.

They requested DHL assist in moving this vehicle on short notice. Anthony Griffin; (DTW) Domestic Operations teamed up with Mick Sheldrake; BDM USGSST Northern Region and advised the US ARMY we “can do it”. In conjunction with Commodore Transportation Group, Vice President and General Manager Hank Willard they orchestrated the loading and direct transport to the Field of Battle, Lincoln Financial Field in Philadelphia, PA to display during the rivalry football game.

The Buffalo mine resistant ambush protected MRAP vehicle was developed by the Force Protection Inc. and weighs 54,000lbs in the stripped down state, the Combat weight is approx 34 Tons. It is a specialized route clearance vehicle used for route-proving and to dispose discovered mines, IEDs and other explosive hazards. The armored vehicle has a V-shaped hull, which directs the force of the blast away from the occupants.

The Army-Navy Game is an annual college football game between the teams of the US Military Academy (USMA) at West Point, NY and the US Naval Academy (USNA) at Annapolis, MD. The game has become an inter-service rivalry of the United States Armed Forces. Navy now leads the all time series with a record of 54 wins, 49 losses, and seven ties. Navy won this year’s game 42-13.

The Marine Corps System Command in Quantico, VA contracted the Plasan Engineering Corp, a global leader in the field of combat-proven ballistic armor solutions for vehicles, to supply the new Marine MRAP’s with the total vehicle protection up armor, which protects against small arms, Improvised Explosive Devices and mines. Since it took a while for the contract amendments to be put into place the entire up armor consignment had to be shipped via air. The Defense Contract Management Agency in Wiesbaden West Germany was given the task of choosing the transportation company with the best solution. DCMA chose DGF and the entire 1,000,000 lbs of freight were put on nine 747 charters and all freight was delivered door to door to final destination in 1 week. This made DCMA and the Marines very happy in that these newly deployed vehicles will be saving lives in the war zone.

The DHL Domestic EUV Group secures a move of an Armored Vehicle Launched Bridge from US Army TACOM in Warren, MI to Anniston Army Depot, Anniston AL. This very extensive domestic move is one of the many services DGF provides to the US ARMY.

The Armored Vehicle Launched Bridge M60A1 is a vehicle used for launching and retrieving a 60-foot scissors-type bridge. Used during combat, an AVLB is a folding portable bridge that is transported on the top of a tank chassis. The bridge and vehicle total weight is 46 tons and when emplaced in less than 2 minutes is capable of supporting tracked and wheeled vehicles with a military load bearing capacity up to Class 60. The roadway width of the AVLB is 12 feet, 6 inches and when unfolded, it can span up to 60 feet while supporting 70 tons of equipment.
