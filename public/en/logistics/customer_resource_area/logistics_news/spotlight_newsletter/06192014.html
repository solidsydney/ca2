The Spotlight Newsletter

 06/19/2014

Update on the U.S. West Coast Longshore Labor Contract

The master contract between the International Longshoremen’s & Warehouse Union (ILWU) and the (employers) Pacific Maritime Association (PMA) will expire at 12:00 AM PDT on June 30. The master contract governs twenty nine ports on the on the U.S. West Coast from San Diego to Seattle covering over 14,000 steady longshoreman.

Since May 12 the PMA and ILWU have conducted daily negotiations. During this time, terminal operations at U.S. West Coast ports have operated normally. While the PMA and ILWU have indicated in updates that negotiations are “positive and progressing,” the outcome of the negotiations and the potential for port disruptions are less than certain. Negotiations are expected to continue past contract expiry.

Outlook:
DHL continues to advise customers to take proactive measures to avoid disruptions in their supply chain should work disruptions occur at U.S. West Coast ports. Congestion and backlog is likely at many container terminals due to the surge in activity prior to the contract expiry. In addition, terminal closures on July 4 and July 5 (in observance of the ILWU Bloody Thursday holiday) will also impact container terminal productivity.

Recommendations

  1. Plan ahead and where possible, expedite cargo through the pipeline and off the ports to minimize the potential for disruptions associated with the contract expiry of the labor contract.
  2. Customers shipping exports from West Coast ports may wish to consider holding back a portion of time sensitive freight for possible air freighting should disruptions occur at U.S. West Coast ports. Containers loaded aboard trains destined to West Coast ports will not be accessible for diversions of cargo.
  3. Utilize air transport as an alternative for critical and urgent needs.
If you have any questions please contact your sales executive or local customer service representative for assistance, they can assist in developing contingency options. DHL will closely monitor the forthcoming negotiations and will provide updates on relevant developments.
