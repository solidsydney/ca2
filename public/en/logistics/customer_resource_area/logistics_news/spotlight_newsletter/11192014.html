The Spotlight Newsletter

 11/19/2014

Congestion Surcharge for Ocean Imports and Exports on U.S. & Canada West Coast Ports

We refer to our previous announcement of a port Congestion Surcharge implementation for cargo moving through the North American West Coast ports.

During meetings and discussions with the Transpacific Stabilization Agreement (TSA) and the Federal Maritime Commission (FMC), questions have been raised as to how and when the announced Congestion Surcharge could apply. This seemingly triggered a reaction on the carrier side with most carriers now having changed their initial position and announcing today that said port Congestion Surcharge has been postponed, pending further review and notice.

What does it mean for you?

Please note that not all carriers have announced a suspension of the Port Congestion Surcharge as each carrier’s FMC tariff rules filings are done independently and can differ widely. As a result, the market remains uncertain and the situation remains very fluid.

Any port Congestion Surcharge imposed by a given carrier not supporting the postponement will be billed as a pass-through charge in accordance with our earlier announcement in order to facilitate cargo release.

DHL expects to see another round of port Congestion Surcharge notices from ocean carriers in the coming days after the carriers have been able to regroup and complete their work to ensure FMC compliance.

What do you need to do?

Should you have any questions, please contact your local DHL Global Forwarding Sales Representative.

If you have containers in transit or delayed at a U.S or Canada West Coast port, please do not hesitate to call your local DHL Global Forwarding contact to discuss shipping alternatives – including our market leading Air Freight services. We look forward to understanding your needs and helping you meet your commitments.

DHL Global Forwarding is monitoring the activities and carrier actions on a continuous basis and will advise on updates as they arise.
