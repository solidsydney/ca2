The Spotlight Newsletter

 02/06/2014

50 Years of Industry Experience in Fashion

There are 30 events worldwide in 15 countries throughout all four continents. The 700 featured designers include Herve Leger by Max Azria, Michael Kors, Vivienne Tam, and Rebecca Minkoff composing 1600 fashion shows and presentations for more than 800,000 visitors each year. This is Mercedes Benz Fashion Week, the lavish display of the latest fashion trends by numerous popular designers.

As the official logistics provider of Mercedes-Benz Fashion Week, DHL provides tailor-made transportation solutions to deliver the latest fashions to runways around-the-world in a timely and efficient manner. With over 50 years of industry experience, DHL provides fully customized, integrated logistics services and complete supply chain management, convenient scheduling, factory-to-store delivery with speedy Customs clearance for Mercedes-Benz Fashion week.

Every designer and fashion house knows that competition hinges on getting the newest trends on the market, not to mention other industry-specific challenges. Have you ever faced difficulties in logistics due to sourcing of raw materials from numerous geographically dispersed markets like Sri Lanka or India? Are you looking for an optimal solution to reduce the pressure of delivering products at competitive prices throughout the value chain and minimizing the risk of the increasing number of seasons per year? Fragmented distribution, global supply chains, inventory management and a shift from physical stores to having an online presence are all specific features that players in the fashion industry are faced with.

With an international presence in 220 countries, DHL has the most extensive fashion and apparel network capabilities, providing full geographical coverage from raw material sources to manufacturing locations in Asia and to consumer markets worldwide. Whether you import the latest collections from Milan, export samples to New Delhi, or set up a distribution network across the globe, DHL ensures your fashions are always in style.

Fashion is a key industry for DHL and as a result it is the official logistics sponsor of Mercedes-Benz Fashion Week. To support the fashion industry worldwide DHL along with IMG just announced the launch of a new program, DHL Exported. The aim is to help designers who are already established in their local market, and are gaining momentum internationally, to further their global footprint via the world’s most powerful fashion week platforms. As part of the DHL Exported program DHL will sponsor a designer for two consecutive seasons and underwrite all logistics costs of producing the collection as well as exporting the collection to New York, London, Milan or Tokyo for the designers’ runway debut.

“DHL has been a longtime partner of fashion weeks worldwide and has continuously supported the advancement of the fashion industry,” said Jarrad Clark, VP and Global Creative Director, IMG Fashion Events and Properties. “DHL Exported stands out from other programs as it aims to help designers who are already locally notable but need that extra boost to further reach their international goals. While there are many successful programs to help emerging talent or designers succeed within one specific country, this program is truly designed with the global mindset that brands today need to adopt in order to grow their brand into international successes.”

With the ever-evolving, competitive and fast paced fashion industry, the need for a supply chain that is as sophisticated as its customers, DHL is continually looking for alternatives and the most innovative services to speedily bring product to market.
