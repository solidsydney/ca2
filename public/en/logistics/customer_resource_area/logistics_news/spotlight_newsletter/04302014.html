The Spotlight Newsletter

 04/30/2014

U.S. West Coast Longshore Labor Contract

The master contract between the International Longshoremen & Warehouse Union (ILWU) and the (employers) Pacific Maritime Association (PMA) will expire on Tuesday, July 1. The master contract governs twenty nine ports on the on the U.S. West Coast from San Diego to Seattle covering over 14,000 steady longshoreman. Combined these ports handle nearly 60% of the containerized cargo imported and exported by the U.S. In the past, contract negotiations have oftentimes been accompanied by disruptions in service and severe congestion within U.S. West Coast ports and the associated transportation infrastructure.

While negotiations between the ILWU and PMA are scheduled to commence on May 12, we urge our customers to proactively evaluate and put in place contingency plans in order to avoid disruptions in operations. Options to consider are:

1. Advance cargo through the pipeline and build safety stock to alleviate the potential for disruptions within the supply chain.

2. Utilize alternative gateways and transloading options via East Coast, Gulf Coast and Canada for cargo well prior to the expiry of the contract to allow time for supply chains to adjust to new routings and transportation nuances.

3. Utilize air transport as an alternative for critical and urgent needs.

DHL is well positioned to manage our customers’ contingency plans with our extensive global ocean network that is complemented by our transloading services, day definite air and ground services, in addition to traditional customs brokerage, FTL and LTL solutions. Our sales and customer service teams are prepared to guide you through the contingency planning process.
Please contact your sales executive or local customer service representative who can assist in developing the contingency options right for your supply chain.

We will closely monitor the forthcoming negotiations and will provide updates on relevant developments.
