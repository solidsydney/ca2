The Spotlight Newsletter

 04/16/2014

CBP's Centers of Excellence & AES Filing Fee Increase

U.S. Customs’ (CBP) Centers of Excellence and Expertise (CEEs) are transforming the way CBP approaches trade operations and works with the international trade community. The Centers represent CBP's expanded focus on “Trade in the 21st Century” by aligning with modern business practices, focusing on industry-specific issues, and by providing tailored support to unique trading environments. The Centers were established to increase uniformity of practices across ports of entry, facilitate the timely resolution of trade compliance issues nationwide and further strengthen critical agency knowledge on key industry practices.

Below are some updates on the CEE initiative:
Certain CEEs To be Given Full Industry Trade Processing Responsibilities
Three CEEs will soon begin to handle processing all entries for their respective industries. Currently only certain companies are allowed to make use of the CEEs. The coming change marks a major step in CBP's plans to open the use of the CEEs to all filers. The Electronics CEE in New York, the Pharmaceutical CEE in Los Angeles and the Petroleum CEE in Houston will become the first centers to handle entries for entire industries.

New Entry Types Eligible for Filing Through CEEs; Now Processing AD/CVD
CBP is now adding the following entry types to those eligible for handling by a CEE (entry must have been filed after participant joined test):

Consumption Entries: Free & Dutiable (Type 01)
Consumption Entries: Appraisement (Type 04)
Consumption Entries: Foreign Trade Zone (Type 06)
Consumption Entries: Duty Deferral (Type 08)
Informal Entries: Free & Dutiable (Type 11)
Temporary Importation under Bond (TIB) (Type 23)
Trade Fair (Type 24)

CBP is also changing its stance on processing AD/CVD (Type 03) consumption entries in CEEs. The CEEs will now process all AD/CVD entries filed during participation in the CEEs test. CBP anticipates it will add additional entry types (quota, informal, warehouse, etc.) in the near future.

CBP has also shifted several products between certain CEEs. Details of these changes may be viewed at: http://www.gpo.gov/fdsys/pkg/FR-2014-03-10/pdf/2014-05115.pdf

Further details on the CEEs are available on the CBP website at:
http://www.cbp.gov/trade/centers-excellence-and-expertise-information

Increase of Automated Export System (AES) Filing Fee
In line with the market, please be informed that DHL Global Forwarding has filed a $3 USD per AES filing increase on Automated Export System Filing (AES) Fee. The AES filing fee will be adjusted to U$28 USD per AES filing effective as of April 28, 2014. The increase will be applied to all customers and all U.S. Ocean Export businesses DHL Global Forwarding handle. Should you have any comments or questions please contact your local sales representative.
