The Spotlight Newsletter

 03/18/2015

New U.S.-Mexico deal facilitates shipping and DHL Trans Border Connect product helps

Have you heard about the deal that will facilitate shipments between the U.S. and Mexico?

October 2014, U.S. Customs and Border Protection and Mexico’s Tax Administration struck a deal that facilitates greater collaboration between the two nations’ trusted trader programs. In effect early 2015, the deal allows for members of trusted trader programs who have submitted themselves to a verification process to move goods between the U.S. and Mexico faster and much more efficiently. The customs agreement guarantees there will be fewer exams when shipping cargo, faster validation processes, transparency between customs’ administrations and more.

In addition to the benefits they can reap from this customs deal, DHL Global Forwarding customers can count on Trans Border Connect to help further their shipping needs. Though especially useful for the automotive, engineering and manufacturing industries, DHL Trans Border Connect promises to give all customers a powerful service with additional advantages.

This all-inclusive product removes any remaining inefficiencies in customers’ supply chains by providing single-source, end-to-end management to move freight across North American borders. Transportation of goods is controlled entirely by DHL Trans Border Connect, from pickup to delivery, which simplifies processes by eliminating multiple carrier bills. Customers can also enjoy lower overall cross-border supply chain costs and an all-in flat fee per pound or kilo, streamlining pricing. Without a doubt, DHL Trans Border Connect is the perfect solution for customers seeking the most efficient service available across the board.

For more information on DHL Global Forwarding’s Trans Border Connect, please contact your local sales representative.
