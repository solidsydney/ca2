Ocean Freight Intermodal Surcharge FDA Reminds Food Facilities to Re-Register by Dec. 31 or Face Import Refusal

FDA Reminds Food Facilities to Re-Register by Dec. 31 or Face Import Refusal

Ocean Freight Intermodal Surcharge

Due to the challenges facing U.S. import and export traffic due to trucking capacity and terminal congestion, Ocean Freight carriers are announcing an Intermodal Door Delivery or Pickup Surcharge for all U.S. deliveries, effective November 15, 2014.

The following are some of the issues causing carriers to
implement this charge:

• Unexpected increases in the cost of inland transport (door pickup/delivery)
• Severe port congestion
• Truck driver shortages
• Increased rail volume

These conditions have had a nationwide impact and are affecting all transportation companies, including carriers, NVOCCs, freight forwarders, and drayage companies.

Due to increased cost in the market, DHL Global Forwarding will be implementing an Intermodal Door Delivery or Pickup Surcharge on all intermodal shipments to or from the U.S.A., effective November 15, 2014.

This surcharge will be applied to all import and export cargo that requires an Intermodal Door-to-Door move.

• Import Intermodal Door Delivery Surcharge: USD 100 per Container
• Export Intermodal Door Pickup Surcharge: USD 100 per Container

DHL Global Forwarding is always mindful of our customers’ interests and is dedicated to providing market-competitive pricing.

Please contact your DHL Global Forwarding representative if you have any questions, or if you would like to discuss how this new surcharge will affect your specific pricing.

Ahmet Erener
Head, Ocean Freight
DHL Global Forwarding
U.S.

FDA Reminds Food Facilities to Re-Register by Dec. 31 or Face Import Refusal

The U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) has issued a reminder that food facilities registered with the FDA must renew their registrations this year between Oct.1 and Dec. 31. The FDA encourages food facilities to renew their registrations early during the three-month renewal period.

The registration requirement applies to domestic and foreign food facilities that manufacture, process, pack, or hold food for human or animal consumption in the United States. Registered facilities must renew their registrations with the FDA every even-numbered year.

Products from food facilities that fail to renew their registrations by Dec. 31 will be subject to import refusal by the FDA.

For more information see:

• Constituent Update: Food Facility Biennial Registration Renewal
• Registration of Food Facilities Main Page
• Information for Startups and Small Entities
• Food Facility Registration User Guide: Biennial Registration Renewal
• Registration Under the FSMA

If you have any questions on this subject, please contact your DHL Global Forwarding representative or chb.consulting@dhl.com.

Paul Vroman
Manager, Regulatory & Compliance Consulting
DHL Global Forwarding
